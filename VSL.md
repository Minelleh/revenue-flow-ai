# Phase 3 · VSL script — Revenue Flow AI

8 minutes. 6 beats. Operator films themselves. No AI avatars. No AI voiceover. No founder story.

Positioning: business infrastructure upgrade. Not another marketing service.

---

## Beat 1 · Hook (0:00 to 0:30)

**Spoken:**

> "Stop waiting for referrals. Install your acquisition engine. If you checked your calendar this week and saw empty slots where sales calls should be, this is for you. Watch the next eight minutes. I will show you what a real pipeline looks like."

**On camera:** Operator face, half-lit. Eye contact direct to lens. No music. Static frame.

**Filming tip:** Sit close to a window with light from one side. Phone on tripod at eye level. Three takes, pick the most direct. The line should feel like a conversation, not a pitch.

---

## Beat 2 · Loop (0:30 to 2:00)

**Spoken:**

> "Here is what most service businesses do. They hire an agency. Run some ads. Buy a CRM. Maybe hire a VA to do cold outreach. Some months are good. Most are not. The pipeline looks full on paper. The calendar tells a different story.
>
> Leads come in. Nobody follows up fast enough. The hot ones go cold in forty-eight hours. The agency says the leads are good. Your sales team says the leads are trash. Everybody points fingers. Nobody books calls.
>
> So you add another tool. Another integration. Another dashboard. Now you have six platforms that do not talk to each other and a pipeline that leaks at every handoff. You are not running a system. You are running a patchwork. And patchwork does not scale. It breaks at two in the morning when a qualified lead replies and nobody is there to book it."

**On camera:** Mix of operator face and B-roll. Screen recordings of a cluttered CRM dashboard, unanswered lead notifications, empty calendar slots. Real or representative screenshots.

**Filming tip:** Capture 30 seconds of a CRM with cold leads, an empty calendar view, and a phone notification from an unanswered lead. Cut between face and screen. The audience should recognize their own dashboard.

---

## Beat 3 · Pivot (2:00 to 3:30)

**Spoken:**

> "The fix is not more leads. That is the trap every agency sells. More leads into a broken system just means more leads that die.
>
> The fix is one system. Outreach, qualification, booking, and follow-up. All connected. All automated. All running whether you are at your desk or not. When a lead comes in, it gets qualified instantly. When it is ready, it gets booked on your calendar. When it goes quiet, it gets followed up. No gaps. No handoffs. No leads rotting in a spreadsheet.
>
> That is what an acquisition engine looks like. Not six tools duct-taped together. One system that does the job."

**On camera:** Operator at their workspace. Could show a clean system diagram or a simple flow on a whiteboard. Keep it real, not polished.

**Filming tip:** If you have a whiteboard, sketch the flow: lead comes in, gets qualified, gets booked, gets followed up. Film yourself walking through it. The simplicity is the proof. If no whiteboard, deliver straight to camera. The conviction carries it.

---

## Beat 4 · Reveal (3:30 to 5:30)

**Spoken:**

> "Here is what Revenue Flow AI installs. Not a campaign. Not a retainer with a slide deck. An acquisition infrastructure with five layers.
>
> Layer one. Outreach. Custom sequences built for your market. Your ideal clients contacted on the channels that convert for your industry. Direct. Specific. Not spray-and-pray.
>
> Layer two. Qualification. Every response scored and filtered in real time. Tire kickers removed before they touch your calendar. Only prospects who match your criteria move forward.
>
> Layer three. Automation. The connective tissue. Every layer talks to the next without human intervention. No manual data entry. No copy-paste between platforms. The system runs itself.
>
> Layer four. Booking. Qualified prospects land directly on your calendar. No back-and-forth emails. No missed windows. The slot fills while you are in another meeting.
>
> Layer five. Follow-up. Automated sequences across email and SMS that re-engage every prospect who did not book the first time. No lead goes cold because someone forgot to reply.
>
> Five layers. One system. You are live in seven days."

**On camera:** Mostly face. A simple numbered list on screen for each layer (white text on dark, minimal). Return to face on every transition. No flashy graphics. The structure sells itself.

**Filming tip:** Break into three recording chunks: layers one and two, layers three and four, layer five plus the close line. Edit with hard cuts. Print the five layers next to the camera for reference. Do not read from a teleprompter.

---

## Beat 5 · Offer (5:30 to 7:30)

**Spoken:**

> "Here is the commitment.
>
> Five hundred dollars. One time. That is the installation fee. I audit your current pipeline, build every layer, configure your system, and launch it. Done in seven days.
>
> One thousand dollars per month. That is the ongoing management. I monitor performance, adjust targeting, update sequences, and make sure your calendar keeps filling. Monthly reporting with real numbers. Direct access to me. Not a support ticket. Not a chatbot. Me.
>
> Total to start: fifteen hundred dollars. For a full acquisition infrastructure that runs on its own.
>
> The guarantee is simple. Qualified appointments on your calendar in ninety days. Your outreach live. Your follow-up automated. Calls landing. If any of those three is not happening in ninety days, I keep working until all three are. No fine print. No conditions. Ninety days."

**On camera:** Face for the full beat. One cut to the website's offer section (screen recording of the pricing card). Mostly conviction on camera.

**Filming tip:** Memorize the numbers. Five hundred. One thousand. Fifteen hundred. Ninety days. Practice three times before recording. The audience needs to hear these numbers land clean. Direct lens contact for the entire two minutes.

---

## Beat 6 · Close (7:30 to 8:00)

**Spoken:**

> "Stop hoping the phone rings. Book the audit. Thirty minutes. I diagnose your pipeline, map your next ninety days, and show you exactly what the system looks like for your business. The link is below."

**On camera:** Operator face. Last shot. Hold the final frame for one beat after the last word.

**Filming tip:** Same setup as Beat 1. Three takes, pick the one with the most direct gaze. End clean. No smile, no nod. Just stop.

## Production rules

- Operator films themselves. Phone, webcam, or DSLR. No AI avatars.
- B-roll = real screen captures. CRM, calendar, system flow. No stock footage.
- No transitions. Hard cuts only.
- No music in the body. Optional ambient room tone.
- No subtitle burn-in. Add CC track on YouTube for accessibility.
- Restrained. Authoritative. Caravaggio not Vegas.
